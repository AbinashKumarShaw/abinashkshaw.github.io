Welcome to my homepage!

I do Cosmology related to understanding various fundamental issues related with the formation and evolution of the very first sources of light in the early Universe. I majorly use the 21-cm radiation, which originates from the neutral atomic hydrogen in its ground state, as a probe to the early Universe. I am a final year Ph.D. student working in the field of Reionization Cosmology under Prof. Somnath Bharadwaj at IIT Kharagpur.

My current studies are focussed on unravelling the important phase transition (from neutral to ionized) of our Universe using theoretical models and simulations of the redshifted 21-cm signal from the Epoch of Reionization. I interpret the reionization 21-cm signal using higher-order Fourier statistics such as Power spectrum, Bispectrum and Trispectrum etc. I also have experience with the Bayesian inference techniques (Fisher matrix, MCMC) which uses the signal statistics for constraining various model parameters. I look forward to developing the statistical tool based pipelines which will be deployed on the real-world 21-cm data. In addition to this, I would like to work on other challenges (foregrounds, systematics etc.) in the field of 21-cm cosmology. Nevertheless, my interests also extends towards exploring the synergies between the 21-cm signal and the other cosmological/astrophysical probes such as the Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation and the Ly-α emitters. The synergies will provide different prespectives to address the above fundamental issues and can revolutionize our current understanding of this field. Last but not the least, I am also interested in the use of machine learning algorithms in astronomy.
